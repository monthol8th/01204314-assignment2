Knowing Africa

In term of population and land mass, Africa is the second largest continent in the world, trailing behind only asia. But, amazingly a majority of the billion people living on the continent survive every day with little to no access to electricity. In the midst of economic, social, and geopolitical turmoil...

Southern Africa

South Africa

South Africa is the 34th largest export economy in the world and the 47th most complex economy according to the Economic Complexity Index The top exports of South Africa are Gold, Diamonds, Platinum, Cars and Coal Briquettes and top imports are Crude Petroleum, Refined Petroleum, Cars, Gold and Broadcasting Equipment.

Northern Africa

Morocco

Morocco is the 67th largest export economy in the world and the 99th most complex economy. The top exports of Morocco are Cars, Mixed Mineral or Chemical Fertilizers, Insulated Wire, Non-Knit Women's Suits and Phosphoric Acid and top imports are Refined Petroleum, Cars, Petroleum Gas, Vehicle Parts and Wheat)

Northern Africa

Egypt

Egypt is the 62nd largest export economy in the world and the 72nd most complex economy.The top exports of Egypt are Crude Petroleum, Gold, Nitrogenous Fertilizers, Refined Petroleum and Insulated Wire and top imports are Wheat, Petroleum Gas, Refined Petroleum , Semi-Finished Iron and Crude Petroleum

Eastern Africa

Kenya

enya is the 101st largest export economy in the world and the 89th most complex economy. The top exports of Kenya are Tea, Cut Flowers, Refined Petroleum, Coffee and Legumes and top imports are Refined Petroleum, Palm Oil, Cars, Packaged Medicaments and Raw Sugar.

Central Africa

Cameroon

Cameroon is the 111th largest export economy in the world and the 106th most complex economy. The top exports of Cameroon are Crude Petroleum, Sawn Wood, Cocoa Beans, Bananas and Rough Wood and top imports are Refined Petroleum, Special Purpose Ships, Rice, Crude Petroleum and Packaged Medicaments.

GDP meaning

Gross domestic product is the total value of everything produced in the country. It doesn't matter if it's produced by are citizens or foreigners. If they are located within the country's boundaries, their production is included in GDP.
